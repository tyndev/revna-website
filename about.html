About Rook

Built by someone who's been in the yard.

Rook didn't start in a product lab. It started at the tail end of a commissioning project — buried in test forms, cross-referencing relay settings at midnight, wondering why the reporting was always the hardest part.

The problem we saw

Commissioning teams are technically excellent. They configure relays, validate protection schemes, and energize substations — work that demands precision and deep expertise.

Then the project wraps, and they spend weeks assembling final reports. Test forms scattered across folders. Photos on three different phones. Client specs that change mid-compilation. The report queue backs up, invoicing stalls, and the next project starts before the last one closes out.

The paperwork shouldn't be the bottleneck. But for most teams, it is.

What we're building

Rook is reporting software purpose-built for electrical testing and commissioning. It takes the raw outputs of your fieldwork — test forms, protection journals, relay settings, photos — and compiles them into client-ready deliverable packages.

No more copy-paste. No more hunting for files. No more rebuilding sections when a client requests a change.

We're talking to commissioning teams right now to build this right. Not guessing at features — listening to the people who live this problem every day.

We're looking for commissioning teams to shape this product.

If you've spent weeks on report closeout, we'd like to hear about it.
