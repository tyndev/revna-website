Frequently Asked Questions

The stuff you'd ask us at the booth.

We're building Revna with input from real T&C teams. Here's what they ask first.

Field Ready

Does it work offline?

Yes. Offline is a hard requirement, not a nice-to-have. Revna runs as a desktop app with a local database on each laptop. Your team can enter test data, run calculations, view plots, and attach files with zero internet. When connectivity returns, everything syncs automatically. If it can't sync, the app tells you — it never silently drops data.

Does it work on our field tablets?

If it runs Windows, yes. Revna is a desktop application that works on Windows laptops, Surface-style tablets, and ruggedized Toughbooks — the same hardware your team already uses in the field. No mobile app needed for the core workflow.

What about photos from phones?

We know field teams snap nameplate photos on their phones — that's not going away. Revna supports importing photos from cloud-synced folders like OneDrive camera roll. Your phone syncs the photo, Revna picks it up on the laptop. No manual file shuffling.

What happens when two people edit the same data offline?

Rare in practice — different team members usually work on different equipment. But when it happens, Revna detects the conflict, shows both values with who entered what and when, and lets a lead pick the right one. No silent overwrites.

Switching & Migration

Do I have to stop using Excel or PowerDB?

For the projects you run through Revna, yes. We've thought hard about this. Trying to keep two systems in sync — someone updating an Excel form on OneDrive while a lead works from Revna — leads to conflicting data and eroded trust. Revna needs to be your single source of truth for the projects you use it on. But you don't have to switch everything overnight. Start with one project, validate it works, then expand.

How do I get my existing templates and data in?

Upload your existing Excel test form templates or export your PowerDB database, and Revna extracts the field definitions, calculations, and structure. You review the extraction, toggle fields on or off, and adjust as needed. The goal is that your team sees their familiar form layout on day one — not a generic template that doesn't match how they work.

Can I start with a project that's already in progress?

Yes. Completed forms get imported with their data, incomplete ones just need the template. You don't have to wait for a fresh project to try Revna.

How long to get my team up and running?

The biggest time investment is the initial template migration — getting your forms into the system. Once that's done, the day-to-day interface is designed to feel familiar to anyone who's filled out test forms in Excel. We're building onboarding to be hands-on: we'll work directly with your team during the pilot to make the transition smooth.

Reports & Customization

Can I customize reports to match what my clients expect?

Yes. Every T&C firm has different client requirements, and we're designing for that from day one. You'll be able to control what sections are included or excluded, toggle fields per-report or per-project, choose between a single PDF package or organized zip folders, and adjust formatting without touching test data. Admins can set company-wide defaults, and leads can override per-project or per-client.

Your Data

What if you go out of business? What happens to my data?

Your data lives on your own machines first — that's the local-first architecture. Even if our servers disappeared tomorrow, every piece of data you've entered is sitting in a database on your laptop. We're also designing data export so you can always pull your information out in standard formats. We're asking you to trust us as your system of record, so we take that seriously.

Is my client data secure?

Data is stored locally on your devices with optional encryption at rest. When it syncs, it travels over encrypted connections to our servers. Every edit is stamped with who changed what and when, giving you a full audit trail. We know T&C firms handle sensitive client infrastructure data and we're building security in from the start, not bolting it on later.

Getting Started

Is this built yet? When can I use it?

We're in the design and architecture phase right now. The product decisions — offline-first, desktop app, local database, automated report assembly — are locked in. What we're doing now is talking to T&C teams to make sure we build the right thing before we build it. We're looking for a small number of pilot partners to work with closely as we develop. If you want to shape this product, now is when your input matters most.

What does the pilot involve?

We'd work directly with your team on a real project. You share your existing forms and workflows, we migrate your templates into Revna, and your team uses it alongside us so we can iterate fast. Pilot partners get direct input on features and priority access as the product develops. We're not looking for hundreds of users — we're looking for a few teams who feel this pain deeply enough to help us get it right.
